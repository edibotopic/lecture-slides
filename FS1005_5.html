  • Rationalism: the view that all knowledge can be obtained through reason

  • Empiricism: the view that all knowledge must be obtained through sense experience


Empiricism is a relatively recent idea in historical terms; although its advantages seem obvious it is not without its problems. A marriage of the rational and empirical is often preferable.

Deception by the Senses



Watch the motion of the blue and yellow objects. They "take turns" stepping forward, like two walking feet. This is more striking if you focus on the space between them. In reality their movement is completely synchronous.
An example of a Gestalt, where the human mind seems to create a pattern that isn't physically there.

  • Reductionism: the view that all knowledge is obtained by breaking the object of study into smaller pieces

  • Determinism: the view that knowledge of the position and momentum of particles is sufficient to predict future events


These are definitely useful epistemological tools but they are not sufficient for describing reality. Complex problems like obesity, viral pandemics and environmental sustainability are difficult to break down like this

As a scientist you strive to be objective, but as a technologist you create things that are always experienced subjectively.
You thus have to keep both ways of thinking about food in mind.

Scientism: the view that all problems should be study scientifically, and that the only valid ideas and explanations arise from the sciences
