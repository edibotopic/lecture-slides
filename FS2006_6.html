Hydrostatic Pressure

$$P = \rho \cdot g \cdot h$$

and

$$P_A = P_S + \rho \cdot g \cdot h$$


Some Consequences

  • The Hydrostatic Paradox (Pascal's barrel)

  • A pressure head can lift a column of liquid

  • There exists absolute and gauge pressure

Height and Pressure


Remember this


In the same way that we say there is an "energy balance" in a system we can also say there is a "pressure balance"


This balance is expressed with Bernoulli's equation

$$\color{violet}{P} + \color{orange}{\rho \cdot \frac{v^2}{2}} + \color{aqua}{\rho \cdot g \cdot h} = constant$$

OR

$$\color{violet}{\frac{P}{\rho \cdot g}} + \color{orange}{\frac{v^2}{2g}} + \color{aqua}{h} = constant$$

OR

$$v = \sqrt{2g\color{aqua}{h}}$$

Toricelli's law states that flow velocity depends on the height of a liquid column.

This does not consider other factors, such as viscosity (Poiseuille's law)
