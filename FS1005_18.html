Conflicts of interest can interfere with our capacity to take responsibility for our role in making decisions with a moral dimension

Do we have an responsibility to limit harm caused by a product you are involved in manufacturing

It is not from the benevolence of the butcher, the brewer, or the baker that we expect our dinner, but from their regard to their own interest

Are you utilitarians?

Most (not all) of you expressed strong utilitarian views, approaching the trolley problem like a cost-benefit analysis. However, your ability to act on this principle was affected by whether the cause-effect was mediated (lever) or not (pushing)
Consider an industrial scientist, who rarely encounters the people that are affected by the technology she makes. The cause-effect here is highly mediated.
Consider further a case where a powerful majority is able to obtain very happy lives by exploiting a less powerful minority - is this a problem?

Consider some of the ideas that are captured by sustainability. Often this involves a moral commitment to abstract classes of beings: "future generations", "the poor", "other genders", "animals".
