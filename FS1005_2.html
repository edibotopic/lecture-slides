Giving Explanations

Question: what is a statue?

Answer: a thing made of stone

Seeking Better Explanations

  • What is it made of. . .
  • How is it made. . .
  • What is its structure. . .
  • Which purpose does it have. . .

Food =

$$matter + process + form + function$$

Aristotle often used crafts (techne) as examples in his ontology. When extended to 'natural' things his theory causes problems. Biologists have largely abandoned the idea of an organism having a purpose (telos) but there is still some value in Aristotle's hylomorphism.

Baconian science

Francis Bacon rejected the formal and final causes, urging scientists to focus on matter and motion.


Interconnectedness

  • If salt isn't present the bread may not last
  • If gluten isn't there the structure may not build
  • If it is baked too short the crust may not brown
  • If the texture is bad the function may fail

Biomimetics: revisited

Natural events (processes) yield desirable structures (forms)

If we could build these (materials) what use (functions) could they have?

At its extreme this leads to skeuomorphism

Thinking narrowly

  • Materialism: food is what it is made of ("matter in motion", "food is a set of nutrients")

  • Functionalism food is what it does ("form follows function", "as long as it tastes good")

Conclusion

Don't think narrowly. . .
