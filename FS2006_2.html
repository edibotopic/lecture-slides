Mass $[M]$

The amount of matter in a body (SI unit: kg)

Typically a given mass is necessary but its weight is contingent

$Mass_{IN} = Mass_{OUT}$


Time $[T]$

Processes are dynamic not static

Note: $1 \ m^3 h^{-1} = 1000 \ kg \ h^{-1}$

A $1.3\% \ solids$ drink enters an evaporator at $30 \ m^3 h^{-1}$ and leaves a 65% solids concentrate



What is the mass flow rate of water and concentrate?

Mass Flow $[M \ T^{-1}]$

$Feed(F) = Concentrate(C) + Water(W)$

$30,000 \ \times \frac{1.3}{100} = (c \ \times \frac{65}{100}) + (w \times \frac{0}{100})$

$30,000 \ \times \frac{1.3}{100} = (c \ \times \frac{65}{100})$

$390 = c \cdot 6.5$

$\frac{390}{6.5} = c$

$c = 600 \ kg \ h^{-1}$

$30,000 - 600 = 29,400$

A tomato is $94\%$ water - half is removed - what is the new water $\%$???


Always remember: $Shelf-life \propto \frac{1}{[water]}$

Dry Basis (why, why, why???)


Concentration often expressed as $kg \ 100kg^{-1} total \ mass$

Sometimes we need to use $kg \ 100kg^{-1} \ solids$



"Water per non-water" seems odd, but can often make more sense
$Tomatoes(T), Dried \ Tomatoes (D), Removed \ Water(R), Content \ of \ Water (c)$

$T = D + R$

$(0.94)(100) = c(D) + (1.0)(47)$

$D = T - R, \ so \ D = 53$

$94 = c53 + 47$

$c = (94-47)/53 = 88.7\%$


On a dry-basis: $94/6 (16\%) \rightarrow \ 47/6 (8\%)$ (more intuitive?)

Length $[L]$, Area $[L^2]$, Volume $[L^3]$


Surface area ($A$) is key in many processes

$Area$ can be estimated from length (e.g., $A = \pi r^2$) and volume ($V$) from area (e.g., $V = \pi r^2.h$)


Typically: $transfer \ rate \propto surface \ area$

Density $[M \ L^{-3}]$

A characteristic property of any material, often used to track process-induced transformations $$\color{orange}{\rho} = \frac{\color{violet}{m}}{\color{aqua}{V}}$$


Density differences ($\Delta \rho$) in food systems are really important

Density

  • Liquids and solids are incompressible
  • Gases are compressible
  • Dispersions of gas are ...?



Powders have two characteristic densities (particle and bulk)

Velocity $[L \ T^{-1}]$


Flow over time in volumetric rate ($Q$) or velocity ($v$)

$$\color{red}{v} = \frac{\color{green}{Q}}{\color{orange}{A}}$$

Velocity $[L \ T^{-1}]$

,Water, Skim milk, Raw milk, Cream Velocity (m/s), 5, 2, 1.5, 0.5

Pressure $[M \ L^{-1} \ T^{-2}]$

Changes in pressure are crucial in many processes

$$P = \frac{F}{A}$$

  • Homogenisation: 30-300 bar
  • Reverse osmosis: 40-80 bar
  • Ultrafiltration: 6-10 bar
  • Evaporation: < 1 bar


Note: $1 bar = 10^5 \ Pa$ and $1 \ atm = 1.013 \ bar$
