The Trolley Problem is a thought experiment designed to probe our moral intuitions, reveal contradictions in our thinking and explore how ethical theories can be applied to help us deal with difficult moral problems.

Does utilitarian scale?

Many express strong utilitarian views, approaching the trolley problem like a cost-benefit analysis. However, people become conflicted when cause-effect is mediated (lever)
Consider an industrial technologist, who rarely encounters the people that they affect The cause-effect is again highly mediated.

Consider some of the ideas that are captured by sustainability. Often this involves a moral commitment to rights and principles: "future generations", "reducing inequality", "decreasing poverty", "non-human life".
