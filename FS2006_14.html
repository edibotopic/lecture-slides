The Basics

  • Distillation is separation based on volatility

  • A binary mixture of $A + B$ is boiled

  • If $T_{bl.}^B < T_{bl.}^A$ the vapor will be enriched in B

  • When vapor condenses it will be enriched in B

Mole fraction in binary mix


$$\color{orange}{X_A = 1-X_B}$$


$$\color{violet}{X_B = 1-X_A}$$



We can infer the level of one from the other

Difference in boiling point


If $T_B >> T_A$ then the "teardrop" is wider

The interactions that occur between molecules are often stronger between similar molecules.
This means that a dilute alcohol solution becomes more difficult to separate as it becomes more concentrated.


Practically, this sets a limit on enrichment
