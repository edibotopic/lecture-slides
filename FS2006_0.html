From Artisinal to Industrial

  • Artisanal design predominated pre-industrial revolution
  • Much depended on tacit (or procedural) knowledge of a master
  • Taylorism accelerated shift from craft- to mass-production
  • Fordism accelerated shift from mass-production to mass-consumption
Breaking process into sub-processes, dividing mastery between workers, standardising the product

Consequences for food processing

The production of commodity goods is driven by common goals: including scale, continuity and quality

Not every food producer operates according to this model, and the industry remains an interesting mixture of tradition and technology (see cheese and whiskey)

Are there still masters?

Yes, those who can design food technologies and continuously optimise their manufacture based on scientific principles and engineering logic
